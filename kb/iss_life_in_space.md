# International Space Station (ISS) & Life in Space

Detailed knowledge about the ISS and human spaceflight for the VR assistant.

## ISS Overview
- The International Space Station is a modular space laboratory orbiting Earth.
- Altitude: approximately 400 km (250 miles) above Earth's surface.
- Orbital speed: ~27,600 km/h (17,150 mph) — completes one orbit every 90 minutes.
- Crew sees 16 sunrises and 16 sunsets every 24 hours.
- Size: roughly the size of a football field (~109m × 73m), including solar arrays.
- Pressurized volume: about the size of a 5-bedroom house (~388 cubic meters).
- Mass: approximately 420,000 kg (925,000 lbs).
- Continuously inhabited since November 2, 2000 — the longest continuous human presence in space.
- A cooperative project among NASA (USA), Roscosmos (Russia), JAXA (Japan), ESA (Europe), and CSA (Canada).

## ISS Construction & History
- First module (Zarya) launched November 20, 1998.
- Built piece by piece over 30+ Space Shuttle missions and Russian Proton/Soyuz launches.
- Has been visited by astronauts from 19 different countries.
- Over 260 people have visited the ISS.
- Originally planned to be deorbited around 2031, with commercial stations taking over.

## Daily Life on the ISS
- Astronauts work about 10 hours per day, 5 days a week, on experiments and maintenance.
- Sleep: crew members sleep in small phone-booth-sized cabins, strapped into sleeping bags.
- Food: mostly freeze-dried or thermostabilized; crew can request favorite foods from home.
- Water: recycled from humidity, urine, and other sources — over 90% of water is reclaimed.
- Toilets: use suction instead of gravity; urine is recycled into drinking water.
- Exercise: mandatory 2 hours per day using a treadmill (COLBERT), cycle ergometer, and resistance device (ARED).
- Communication: crew can email, make phone calls, and do video conferences with family.
- The ISS has WiFi and a small library of movies and books.

## ISS Science
- Over 3,000 scientific experiments conducted across biology, physics, medicine, Earth observation, and technology.
- Protein crystal growth: crystals grow larger and more perfect in microgravity, helping drug development.
- Plant growth experiments: lettuce, radishes, and peppers have been grown and eaten on the ISS.
- Fire behavior: flames in microgravity are spherical and burn differently, teaching us about combustion.
- Fluid physics: studying how liquids behave without gravity helps design better fuel tanks and life support.
- Earth observation: astronauts photograph natural disasters, urban growth, coral reef health, and more.
- Dark matter detection: the Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer (AMS-02) has detected billions of cosmic rays.

## ISS Fun Facts
- The ISS is the 3rd brightest object in the night sky (after the Sun and Moon).
- You can see it from Earth with the naked eye — it looks like a fast-moving bright star.
- Astronauts can see the Great Wall of China from the ISS (contrary to the myth, you CAN with good conditions).
- The ISS travels fast enough to travel from London to New York in about 13 minutes.
- Crew members' fingernails sometimes fall off due to spacesuit glove pressure during spacewalks.
- Pizza has been delivered to the ISS (Pizza Hut paid $1 million to send a pizza in 2001).
- The ISS has hosted a film crew — the first movie filmed in space was a Russian production in 2021.

## Spacewalks (EVA - Extravehicular Activity)
- Over 260 spacewalks have been conducted from the ISS.
- Average duration: 6-7 hours.
- Spacesuits (EMU) weigh about 127 kg on Earth but are weightless in orbit.
- Astronauts wear a maximum absorbency garment (space diaper) during spacewalks.
- The suit provides oxygen, removes CO₂, maintains temperature, and protects against micrometeoroids.
- Astronauts use tethers to stay attached to the station — there's no "coming back" if you float away.
- There is a jetpack (SAFER) as an emergency backup, but it's never been used for real.

## Future of Space Stations
- NASA planning transition to commercial space stations (Axiom Station, Orbital Reef, Starlab).
- China's Tiangong space station has been operational since 2022 with 3-person crew.
- The ISS is scheduled for a controlled deorbiting, likely splashing down in the remote South Pacific.
- SpaceX has been contracted to build the ISS deorbit vehicle.
- Future stations may orbit the Moon (NASA's Lunar Gateway is under construction for Artemis missions).
