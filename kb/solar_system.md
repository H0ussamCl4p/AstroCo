# Solar System Knowledge Base

Compact, retrieval-friendly notes for the VR astronomy assistant.

## The Sun
- Type: G2V main-sequence star (yellow dwarf).
- Age: approximately 4.6 billion years old.
- Diameter: about 1.39 million km (109× Earth's diameter).
- Surface temperature: ~5,500°C (9,932°F).
- Core temperature: ~15 million°C.
- Composition: ~73% hydrogen, ~25% helium, ~2% heavier elements.
- The Sun contains 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
- Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes 20 seconds to reach Earth.
- The Sun will eventually become a red giant in about 5 billion years.

## Mercury
- Closest planet to the Sun; orbital period ~88 Earth days.
- No atmosphere to speak of (extremely thin exosphere).
- Temperature extremes: −180°C at night to 430°C during the day.
- Diameter: ~4,879 km (smallest planet).
- No moons, no rings.
- Surface covered in craters, similar to Earth's Moon.
- MESSENGER spacecraft orbited Mercury 2011–2015; BepiColombo (ESA/JAXA) en route.

## Venus
- Second planet from the Sun; similar size to Earth ("Earth's twin").
- Diameter: ~12,104 km.
- Thick CO₂ atmosphere with sulfuric acid clouds; extreme greenhouse effect.
- Surface temperature: ~465°C (hotter than Mercury despite being farther from the Sun).
- Surface pressure: ~92 atmospheres (equivalent to ~900m underwater on Earth).
- Rotates backwards (retrograde) and very slowly; one Venus day ≈ 243 Earth days.
- No moons, no rings.

## Earth
- Third planet from the Sun; only known planet with life.
- Diameter: ~12,742 km.
- One natural satellite: the Moon.
- 71% of surface covered by water.
- Atmosphere: ~78% nitrogen, ~21% oxygen.
- Magnetic field protects from solar wind and cosmic radiation.
- Orbital period: 365.25 days; axial tilt: 23.4° (causes seasons).

## The Moon (Earth's Moon / Luna)
- Earth's only natural satellite.
- Diameter: ~3,474 km (about 1/4 of Earth's diameter).
- Distance from Earth: average ~384,400 km.
- Tidally locked: same face always points toward Earth.
- No atmosphere (technically a very thin exosphere).
- Surface: craters, maria (dark basaltic plains), highlands.
- Formed ~4.5 billion years ago (giant impact hypothesis: a Mars-sized body called Theia).
- Apollo program: 6 crewed landings (1969–1972); 12 humans walked on the Moon.
- South pole: permanently shadowed craters may contain water ice; target for Artemis missions.
- Gravity: ~1/6 of Earth's (1.62 m/s²).

## Mars
- Fourth planet; the "Red Planet" (iron oxide on surface).
- Diameter: ~6,779 km (about half of Earth).
- Thin CO₂ atmosphere; surface pressure ~0.6% of Earth's.
- Surface temperature: average −60°C.
- Has two small moons: Phobos and Deimos.
- Olympus Mons: largest volcano in the solar system (~21.9 km tall).
- Valles Marineris: largest canyon system (~4,000 km long, up to 7 km deep).
- Evidence of ancient water: river channels, mineral deposits, polar ice caps.
- Active rovers: Curiosity (since 2012), Perseverance (since 2021).
- Perseverance carries Ingenuity helicopter (first powered flight on another planet).
- Mars is a primary target for future human missions (NASA Moon-to-Mars plan).

## Jupiter
- Fifth planet; largest in the solar system.
- Diameter: ~139,820 km (11× Earth).
- Mass: 318× Earth (more than all other planets combined).
- Gas giant: no solid surface; atmosphere mostly hydrogen and helium.
- Great Red Spot: giant storm larger than Earth, raging for centuries.
- At least 95 known moons.
- Galilean moons (discovered by Galileo, 1610):
  - Io: most volcanically active body in the solar system.
  - Europa: ice-covered ocean world; prime candidate for extraterrestrial life.
  - Ganymede: largest moon in the solar system; has its own magnetic field.
  - Callisto: heavily cratered; possible subsurface ocean.
- Faint ring system discovered by Voyager 1 in 1979.
- Juno spacecraft orbiting since 2016.

## Saturn
- Sixth planet; famous for its spectacular ring system.
- Diameter: ~116,460 km (9.5× Earth).
- Gas giant: density less than water (would float if you had a big enough bathtub).
- Ring system: mainly water ice and rock; extends up to 282,000 km from center but only ~10m thick.
- At least 146 known moons.
- Titan: second-largest moon in solar system; has thick nitrogen atmosphere and liquid methane lakes.
- Enceladus: ice moon with geysers; subsurface ocean; potential for life.
- Cassini-Huygens mission (1997–2017) revolutionized Saturn science.

## Uranus
- Seventh planet; ice giant.
- Diameter: ~50,724 km.
- Rotates on its side (axial tilt ~98°); possibly from ancient collision.
- Atmosphere: hydrogen, helium, methane (gives blue-green color).
- 27 known moons (named after Shakespeare and Alexander Pope characters).
- Faint ring system.
- Visited only once: Voyager 2 flyby in 1986.
- A dedicated Uranus orbiter mission is a top priority in planetary science.

## Neptune
- Eighth and farthest planet from the Sun.
- Diameter: ~49,528 km.
- Ice giant: similar composition to Uranus.
- Strongest winds in the solar system: up to 2,100 km/h.
- 16 known moons; Triton is the largest (geologically active, retrograde orbit).
- Faint ring system.
- Visited only once: Voyager 2 flyby in 1989.

## Dwarf Planets
- Pluto: reclassified as dwarf planet in 2006; New Horizons flyby in 2015 revealed complex geology and a thin atmosphere.
- Ceres: largest object in the asteroid belt; Dawn spacecraft orbited 2015–2018.
- Eris, Makemake, Haumea: trans-Neptunian dwarf planets.

## Asteroid Belt
- Located between Mars and Jupiter.
- Contains millions of rocky bodies; total mass less than Earth's Moon.
- Largest object: Ceres (dwarf planet, ~940 km diameter).
- Most asteroids are irregular in shape; some have moons.
- DART mission (2022): NASA successfully changed asteroid Dimorphos's orbit (planetary defense test).

## Comets
- Icy bodies from the outer solar system; develop tails when near the Sun.
- Short-period comets: originate from the Kuiper Belt (e.g., Comet Halley, ~76 year orbit).
- Long-period comets: originate from the Oort Cloud.
- Rosetta mission (ESA): first to orbit and land on a comet (67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko, 2014).
