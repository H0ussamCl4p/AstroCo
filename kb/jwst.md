# James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) — Discoveries & Capabilities

Detailed knowledge about JWST for the VR astronomy assistant.

## JWST Overview
- Full name: James Webb Space Telescope, named after former NASA administrator James E. Webb.
- Launched: December 25, 2021 (Christmas Day) on an Ariane 5 rocket from French Guiana.
- Cost: approximately $10 billion — the most expensive space telescope ever built.
- Development time: over 20 years (concept started in 1996).
- Collaboration between NASA, ESA, and CSA (Canadian Space Agency).
- Location: orbits the Sun at the second Lagrange point (L2), about 1.5 million km from Earth.
- At L2, the telescope always has the Sun, Earth, and Moon behind it, keeping instruments cold.

## Mirror & Design
- Primary mirror: 6.5 meters across (2.7x larger than Hubble's 2.4m mirror).
- Made of 18 hexagonal gold-coated beryllium segments that unfold after launch.
- Gold coating reflects infrared light efficiently (only 48.25 grams of gold used).
- Sunshield: 5-layer, tennis-court-sized shield made of Kapton; keeps instruments at −233°C.
- The sunshield's sun-facing side can reach 85°C while the dark side stays at −233°C.
- JWST observes primarily in infrared light, unlike Hubble which focuses on visible and UV light.

## Why Infrared?
- Infrared light passes through cosmic dust clouds that block visible light, revealing hidden stars and galaxies.
- The most distant objects in the universe have their light stretched (redshifted) into infrared by cosmic expansion.
- JWST can see galaxies from just 200-300 million years after the Big Bang (13.5 billion years ago).
- Infrared is also key for studying exoplanet atmospheres — molecules like water, CO₂, and methane absorb specific infrared wavelengths.

## Key Instruments
- NIRCam (Near-Infrared Camera): primary imager; takes stunning photos of galaxies, nebulae, and star-forming regions.
- NIRSpec (Near-Infrared Spectrograph): can analyze light from 100 objects simultaneously; studies chemical compositions.
- MIRI (Mid-Infrared Instrument): sees deeper into dust; can study cooler objects like debris disks and distant galaxies.
- FGS/NIRISS (Fine Guidance Sensor / Near-Infrared Imager and Slitless Spectrograph): Canadian instrument for precision pointing and exoplanet observations.

## Major Discoveries & Results

### Earliest Galaxies
- JWST discovered galaxies existing just 300-400 million years after the Big Bang.
- Some of these early galaxies are surprisingly massive and well-formed, challenging existing theories of galaxy formation.
- The galaxy JADES-GS-z14-0 was confirmed as one of the most distant galaxies ever observed (~290 million years after the Big Bang).

### Exoplanet Atmospheres
- First direct detection of CO₂ in an exoplanet atmosphere (WASP-39b).
- Detected water vapor, sulfur dioxide, and clouds in exoplanet atmospheres.
- Studying TRAPPIST-1 system — 7 Earth-sized planets, some in the habitable zone.
- Can potentially detect biosignature gases (like methane + oxygen together) in rocky exoplanet atmospheres.

### Star Formation
- Revealed intricate structures in stellar nurseries like the Carina Nebula ("Cosmic Cliffs").
- Showed detailed views of protoplanetary disks where planets are forming around young stars.
- Discovered new details about how stars form in the Pillars of Creation (Eagle Nebula, M16).

### Our Solar System
- Captured stunning images of Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune in infrared.
- Revealed previously unseen details of Jupiter's aurora and ring system.
- Detected water vapor plumes on Saturn's moon Enceladus extending 10,000 km into space.
- Studied asteroids and Kuiper Belt objects in unprecedented detail.

## JWST vs Hubble
- JWST mirror is 6.5m vs Hubble's 2.4m (6.25× the collecting area).
- JWST sees infrared; Hubble sees visible + UV + some near-infrared.
- JWST is 1.5 million km from Earth; Hubble orbits at ~540 km altitude.
- Hubble can be repaired by astronauts; JWST cannot (too far away).
- JWST expected lifetime: 20+ years (originally designed for 10, but fuel efficiency at launch extended this).
- They complement each other — Hubble is still operating and doing great science.

## JWST Fun Facts
- The telescope had to unfold like origami after launch — 344 single points of failure, all worked perfectly.
- JWST's sunshield SPF rating is about 1 million.
- The telescope is so sensitive it could detect the heat signature of a bumblebee on the Moon.
- First full-color images released July 12, 2022 — President Biden unveiled the first image.
- JWST will transform our understanding of the universe for decades to come.
