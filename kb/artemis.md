# Artemis Program (RAG Notes)

Compact, retrieval-friendly notes to keep the assistant grounded on NASA’s Artemis lunar exploration program.
Schedules and details can change; treat future mission dates as *planning targets* unless a flight has occurred.

## Artemis (program overview)
- Artemis is NASA’s campaign to send humans back to the Moon and build the capabilities and partnerships needed for sustained exploration.
- Rationale (as described by NASA): scientific discovery, economic benefits, and inspiring a new generation, while building toward longer-term human exploration goals.
- Program style: “increasingly complex missions” that step from flight tests → crewed deep-space operations → lunar surface missions.

## Artemis Accords (policy / partnership context)
- The Artemis Accords are a set of principles for civil exploration and use of space.
- Established in 2020 (NASA + U.S. Department of State + initial partner nations).
- NASA’s Artemis overview states there are now 60+ signatories.

## Key systems / elements (what the stack is)
- Space Launch System (SLS): NASA’s super heavy-lift rocket for sending Orion (plus crew/cargo) beyond Earth orbit.
- Orion: NASA’s crew spacecraft for deep-space travel; designed to support the crew, provide abort capability, and return safely at high lunar-return reentry speeds.
- Exploration Ground Systems (EGS): Kennedy Space Center ground infrastructure that processes/launches Artemis vehicles and supports post-flight recovery operations.
- Human Landing System (HLS) / lunar lander: the lunar descent/ascent system used after arriving in lunar orbit.
- Moon surface EVA suits: NASA’s Artemis mission pages describe Axiom Space’s advanced suit being used for moonwalks on later surface missions.

## Artemis missions (quick map)
- Artemis I (FLIGHTED): first integrated uncrewed SLS + Orion flight test; Moon mission and Earth return.
- Artemis II (PLANNED; first crewed flight): crewed lunar flyby / around-the-Moon mission to prove crewed deep-space capability.
- Artemis III (PLANNED): NASA’s Artemis overview describes it as a new low Earth orbit demonstration mission to test one or both commercial landers.
- Artemis IV (PLANNED): crewed mission that includes a lunar surface stay near the Moon’s south pole region.
- Artemis V (PLANNED): NASA overview describes standardizing SLS configuration and aiming for roughly annual cadence for later surface missions.

## Artemis I (mission facts + why it mattered)

### Artemis I (FLIGHTED)
- Mission type (NASA): uncrewed lunar flight test.
- Purpose: first integrated flight test of the deep-space stack (SLS + Orion + ground systems) before flying astronauts.
- Launch: Nov 16, 2022.
- Splashdown: Dec 11, 2022.
- Duration: 25 days, 10 hours, 53 minutes.
- Total distance traveled: ~1.4 million miles.
- Re-entry speed: 24,581 mph (about Mach 32).
- Trajectory summary: Orion flew beyond the Moon and returned for high-energy reentry and Pacific Ocean splashdown.

### Artemis I (RAG snippets)
- “Artemis I” = first integrated SLS/Orion flight test; uncrewed; lunar-distance mission; returned via high-speed reentry.
- “Why it matters” = data and confidence for the first crewed Orion mission.

## Artemis II (mission facts + crew)

### Artemis II (PLANNED)
- Mission type (NASA): crewed lunar flyby.
- Crew size: 4.
- Duration (NASA): about 10 days.
- Goal: first mission to fly astronauts on SLS + Orion; demonstrate operations needed for deep-space crewed missions by sending the crew around the Moon.
- Launch timing (as listed on NASA mission page): no earlier than April 1, 2026.

### Artemis II (late March 2026 pre-launch updates — media reporting)
- Context: news coverage reported the Artemis II crew arriving at Kennedy Space Center (KSC) and visiting the launch site ahead of an early-April attempt.
- Arrival reported: Fri, Mar 27, 2026.
- Crew reported present (matches NASA public crew list): Reid Wiseman (commander), Victor Glover (pilot), Christina Koch (mission specialist), Jeremy Hansen (mission specialist, Canada).
- Reported details:
	- The crew flew in from Houston and arrived in NASA T-38 training jets.
	- Media described this as the crew’s first time being “this close” to the launch.
	- Reporting mentioned recent technical issues (e.g., fuel leaks) contributing to schedule slips and rollouts between the processing building and pad.
- Launch window (media reporting): NASA targeting as early as Wed (early April), with additional daily opportunities through the first days of April, then a pause before later opportunities.
- Important note for the assistant: treat these as *time-sensitive* and *reporting-dependent*; for “what is the official current launch date,” prefer NASA’s mission page / live updates.

### Artemis II (early April 2026 mission explainers — media reporting)
- Context: additional media explainers described Artemis II as the first crewed Artemis mission and highlighted what the flight is meant to prove.
- Launch period described (media): a targeted window in early April 2026 (often summarized as Apr 1–Apr 6), subject to technical readiness and weather.
- Mission profile described (media):
	- Initial checkout in Earth orbit.
	- Translunar injection toward the Moon, with a ~3–4 day outbound coast described.
	- Lunar flyby / around-the-Moon flight (no landing).
	- Return to Earth for high-energy reentry and Pacific Ocean splashdown.
- What Artemis II is testing (media): life support / crew systems, navigation, communications, and heat-shield performance on a crewed deep-space return.
- Program staging (media): some reporting frames Artemis II as the crewed systems test before a later crewed landing mission.
- “Firsts” highlighted in media:
	- Christina Koch described as the first woman to fly on a crewed mission around the Moon.
	- Victor Glover described as the first Black astronaut to travel toward/around the Moon.
	- Jeremy Hansen described as the first non-American to travel beyond low Earth orbit toward the Moon.
- Important note for the assistant: treat these as *press/reporting claims* and verify with NASA bios/press kits if the user asks for “official firsts” language.

## Artemis IV (mission facts + surface operations)

### Artemis IV (PLANNED)
- Mission type (NASA mission page): crewed surface landing.
- Launch timing (as listed on NASA mission page): early 2028.
- High-level mission flow (NASA):
	- Four astronauts launch in Orion on SLS from Kennedy Space Center.
	- Orion travels to lunar orbit and docks with a lander.
	- Two crew members transfer to the Human Landing System, descend to the surface, conduct surface work, then return to lunar orbit.
- Surface stay (NASA): approximately one week near the lunar south pole region.
- Surface activities (NASA): sample collection and science work; observation of the lunar environment.
- EVA suits (NASA): Axiom Space’s advanced spacesuit is described as the suit used for moonwalks on this mission profile.
- Site context (NASA): NASA has identified candidate landing regions near the lunar south pole for Artemis surface missions.

### Artemis IV (RAG snippets)
- “Artemis IV” = crewed surface landing mission; south pole region; ~1 week surface stay; 2 astronauts descend via HLS.

## Science + ops themes (common answers)
- South pole interest: permanently shadowed regions and polar volatiles are a major driver for site selection and science.
- Enablers: comms/navigation and reliable logistics are repeatedly emphasized by NASA as critical for safe and productive lunar operations.
