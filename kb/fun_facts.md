# Fun Space Facts & Festival Conversation Starters

Bite-sized, engaging astronomy facts perfect for a space festival audience.

## Mind-Blowing Scale Facts
- If the Sun were the size of a basketball, Earth would be a small pea about 26 meters away.
- Light from the Sun takes 8 minutes 20 seconds to reach Earth, but over 4 years to reach the next closest star.
- The observable universe is about 93 billion light-years across.
- There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all Earth's beaches (~2 × 10²³ stars estimated).
- A teaspoon of neutron star material would weigh about 6 billion tons.
- If you could drive a car at highway speed straight up, you'd reach space in about 1 hour.
- The Milky Way and Andromeda galaxies are on a collision course; they'll merge in about 4.5 billion years.

## Space Records
- Fastest human-made object: Parker Solar Probe — over 635,000 km/h (0.06% speed of light).
- Farthest human-made object: Voyager 1 — over 24 billion km from Earth (launched 1977, still sending data).
- Longest continuous time in space: Valeri Polyakov — 437 days aboard Mir space station (1994–1995).
- Most distant photo of Earth: "Pale Blue Dot" by Voyager 1 from 6 billion km away (1990).
- Oldest light ever detected: cosmic microwave background radiation — 13.8 billion years old.
- Coldest known place in the universe: Boomerang Nebula at −272°C (1° above absolute zero).

## Surprising Facts
- Space is completely silent — there's no air to carry sound waves.
- Astronauts grow 2-5 cm taller in microgravity because the spine decompresses.
- The footprints on the Moon will last millions of years — there's no wind or water to erode them.
- A day on Venus (243 Earth days) is longer than a year on Venus (225 Earth days).
- Olympus Mons on Mars is so large that standing at its base, you wouldn't know you're on a mountain — it curves with the planet.
- Saturn's density is so low it would float in water (if you had a big enough pool).
- Jupiter's Great Red Spot has been raging for at least 300 years and could fit 2-3 Earths inside it.
- You can fit all the other planets in our solar system between Earth and the Moon (average distance).
- Neutron stars can spin at up to 716 rotations per second.
- One million Earths could fit inside the Sun.

## Space & The Human Body
- In space, without gravity, flames are spherical and blue (not teardrop-shaped).
- Tears don't fall in space — they pool into a ball on your face.
- Food tastes blander in microgravity because fluids shift to the head, causing stuffiness.
- Astronauts' hearts become more spherical in microgravity.
- The ISS has a gym — astronauts exercise 2 hours per day to prevent muscle and bone loss.
- Cosmic radiation exposure over a Mars mission (~3 years) is a significant health concern.

## Mythology & Culture
- Almost every human civilization has created stories about the stars.
- The word "planet" comes from Greek "planetes" meaning "wanderer" — because planets move relative to fixed stars.
- The word "astronaut" means "star sailor" (Greek: astron + nautes).
- The word "cosmonaut" means "universe sailor" (Greek: kosmos + nautes).
- Many constellation names come from Greek myths, but indigenous cultures worldwide have their own star stories.
- The Aboriginal Australians have some of the oldest astronomical traditions, over 65,000 years old.
- The Dogon people of Mali knew about Sirius B (a white dwarf invisible to naked eye) long before Western astronomers confirmed it.

## Numbers That Amaze
- Speed of light: 299,792 km/s (fast enough to circle Earth 7.5 times in one second).
- Age of the universe: ~13.8 billion years.
- Age of Earth: ~4.5 billion years.
- Number of galaxies in observable universe: estimated 200 billion to 2 trillion.
- Distance to nearest galaxy (Andromeda): ~2.5 million light-years.
- Temperature of the Sun's core: ~15 million °C.
- Size of the largest known star (UY Scuti or Stephenson 2-18): ~2,000 times the Sun's radius.
